# Pinterest Selection Guide for TikTok AI Model Videos

This guide helps you select the best Pinterest reference images for generating AI models wearing your products.

---

## Why Pinterest References Matter

Pinterest provides:
- **Visual inspiration**: Real poses, angles, lighting
- **Style direction**: Brand aesthetic, mood, atmosphere
- **Quality benchmark**: High-quality photography standards
- **Creative ideas**: Unique poses and compositions

Good references = Better AI results

## What Makes a Good Reference Image

### ✅ Good References

**Pose Characteristics**:
- Clear, visible pose (arms, legs, head position)
- Natural body language
- Product-compatible stance (e.g., holding item, wearing it)
- Not too complex or awkward

**Lighting Quality**:
- Even illumination
- Visible light direction
- No harsh shadows on face/body
- Professional studio look

**Camera Angle**:
- Clear perspective (front, side, profile)
- Appropriate for product (close-up for jewelry, full body for fashion)
- Eye-level or slightly above eye-level
- Not distorted by wide-angle lens

**Composition**:
- Subject clearly visible
- No distracting elements
- Balanced framing
- Focus on model + product integration

**Style Match**:
- Matches your brand aesthetic
- Suitable for target audience
- Professional quality
- Consistent with your other content

### ❌ Bad References

**Avoid**:
- Extremely complex poses (hard for AI to replicate)
- Extreme angles (distorted perspective)
- Poor lighting (dark, harsh shadows)
- Blurry or low-quality images
- Distracting backgrounds
- Overly stylized or avant-garde (unless that's your brand)
- Images with text/overlays
- Low-resolution pins

## Pinterest Selection Checklist

When selecting a reference, check:

### Pose & Angle
- [ ] Pose is clear and natural
- [ ] Body position matches product type
- [ ] Camera angle shows product well
- [ ] Not too complex for AI generation

### Lighting & Quality
- [ ] Professional lighting
- [ ] No harsh shadows on face
- [ ] High resolution
- [ ] No blur or distortion

### Style & Brand
- [ ] Matches your brand aesthetic
- [ ] Fits target audience
- [ ] Professional commercial quality
- [ ] Consistent with your content

### Technical
- [ ] Vertical or horizontal (match TikTok orientation)
- [ ] No text overlays
- [ ] Clean background (easier to replicate)
- [ ] Clear subject focus

## Batch Selection Strategy

### For Multiple Products
1. **Save 3-5 diverse references** per product
2. **Mix poses** (front, side, detail shots)
3. **Test all** to see which AI generates best
4. **Reuse successful** references for similar products

### For Product Variants
1. **Select same reference** for color variants
2. **Keep consistent** brand aesthetic
3. **Minor prompt adjustments** only (color, material)

### For Seasonal Collections
1. **Update reference style** per season
2. **Spring/Summer**: Light, bright, outdoor
3. **Fall/Winter**: Warm, cozy, indoor

## Workflow Integration

### Before Starting
1. Browse Pinterest and save 2-3 references
2. Analyze each with checklist above
3. Choose best reference
4. Note pose, angle, lighting details

### During Prompt Creation
5. Include Pinterest analysis in Claude prompt:
   "Use this Pinterest image as reference for pose (standing upright), angle (full body), lighting (studio)"

### After Generation
6. Compare AI result to Pinterest reference
7. If mismatch, refine prompt with more specific details
8. Save successful prompt-template combinations

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Choosing Too Complex Poses
**Problem**: Acrobatic, extreme, or highly stylized poses
**Result**: AI fails to replicate, unnatural output
**Solution**: Choose simple, natural poses first

### ❌ Mistake 2: Ignoring Lighting
**Problem**: Selecting reference without analyzing lighting
**Result**: Generated image doesn't match reference style
**Solution**: Note lighting direction (studio, natural, dramatic)

### ❌ Mistake 3: Mismatched Brand Aesthetic
**Problem**: Choosing trendy/edgy reference for minimalist brand
**Result**: Inconsistent brand identity
**Solution**: Always align with brand style

### ❌ Mistake 4: Low-Quality References
**Problem**: Saving blurry or small images
**Result**: AI quality limited by reference quality
**Solution**: Always choose high-resolution, professional pins
